Neuropharmacology Research Internship

During my internship at the City University of New York, I conducted research in neuropharmacology and cell biology, applying advanced techniques like centrifugation and homogenization to extract organelles from mouse brain tissue, and cultivating B-35 neuronal cells under optimal conditions. I investigated the effects of the drug SKF 38393 on CDPDG production through radiolabeling and scintillation counting, and studied spinal cord injury recovery challenges, focusing on glial scar tissue's role in inhibiting nerve regeneration. This experience honed my lab skills in cell culture, enzymatic cell detachment, and radioactivity analysis, while deepening my understanding of experimental design and data interpretation.
